Dr. Ameyo Stella Adadevoh

The woman who curbed the wide spread of Ebola Virus in Nigeria

Here's a timeline of Dr. Adadevoh's life:

  • 1956 - Born in Lagos, Nigeria in October.
  • 1962 - Finished preschool at the Mainland Preparatory Primary School in Yaba, Lagos.
  • 1968 - Attended primary school at the Corona School, Yaba in Lagos, Nigeria.
  • 1974 - Finished secondary school education at Queen's School, Ibadan, Nigeria.
  • 1980 - Graduated from the University of Lagos College of Medicine with a Bachelor of Medicine/Bachelor of Surgery.
  • 1981 - Served her one-year mandatory housemanship at Lagos University Teaching Hospital
  • 1983 - Spent her residency at Lagos University Teaching Hospital and obtained her West African College of Physicians and Surgeons credential.
  • 1986 - She married Afolabi Emmanuel Cardoso
  • 2012 - Adadevoh was the first to alert the Nigerian Ministry of Health when H1N1 spread to Nigeria.
  • 2014 - Adadevoh correctly diagnosed Liberian Patrick Sawyer as Nigeriaâ€™s first case of Ebola at First Consultant Hospital in Lagos, Nigeria.
  • 2014 - She died from the Ebola virus in quarantine in Lagos, Nigeria
  • 2018 - A movie 93 Days is dedicated to Adadevoh and tells the story of the treatment of Sawyer by Adadevoh and other medical staff at First Consultant Medical Center.
